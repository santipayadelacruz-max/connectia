LinkedIn is expensive per click and that's by design — you're paying for precision targeting that no other platform can match for B2B. The question isn't whether the CPC is high, it's whether the leads it generates are worth more than what you'd pay elsewhere for similar quality.

For most B2B businesses we work with, the answer is yes — but only when the targeting is genuinely tight. Broad job title targeting wastes budget fast. We narrow by company size, seniority and specific role combinations, accepting a smaller audience in exchange for far less wasted spend.

Lead Gen Forms consistently outperform sending LinkedIn traffic to an external landing page, simply because the friction of leaving the platform and filling in another form kills conversion rate. We default to forms unless there's a specific reason not to.

FAQ

Common questions

LinkedIn CPCs are often €5–15 per click. It's worth it if you're targeting specific professional roles and lead value justifies the cost. For B2B with deal sizes above €5k–10k, LinkedIn often delivers better quality leads than cheaper channels.

Depends on your goal. Single image ads work well for direct response. Lead Gen Forms reduce friction and often outperform landing page traffic. Document ads are growing fast for thought leadership.

LinkedIn audiences are smaller so the learning phase takes longer — 3–4 weeks per campaign. Budget for testing: you need runway to find what works.
