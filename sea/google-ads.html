Google Ads punishes set-and-forget management more than any other channel — the auction changes constantly, and an account left untouched for a month is usually bleeding budget on queries that stopped converting weeks ago. We check campaigns weekly at minimum, more often during active testing periods.

The biggest lever most accounts are missing isn't a clever bidding strategy — it's clean account structure. Tightly themed ad groups, accurate match types, a negative keyword list that's actually maintained. Smart bidding works far better when it's working with clean data, not compensating for messy structure.

We're upfront that Performance Max isn't always the answer Google's sales team suggests it is. It performs well with strong creative assets and enough conversion volume. Without those, more controlled campaign types usually deliver better, more transparent results.
