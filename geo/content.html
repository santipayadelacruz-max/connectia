AI engines don't read content the way humans browse a page — they extract specific answers from specific sections. Content that buries the actual answer in paragraph four after three paragraphs of context tends not to get cited. Content that states the answer clearly, then explains it, performs better.

This pushes us toward formats that might have felt clinical a few years ago — clear headers that state exactly what the section answers, FAQ blocks, numbered steps, definitions stated plainly before being expanded on. It's not radically different from good SEO writing. It's a sharper version of the same discipline.

We also pay attention to E-E-A-T signals more deliberately than we might for a piece purely targeting Google — clear authorship, demonstrated first-hand experience, citing primary sources rather than other secondary content. These signals matter for both disciplines, but GEO seems to weight them even more heavily.
