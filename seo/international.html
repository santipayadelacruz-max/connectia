International SEO is where good intentions go wrong most often. A business expands into a new market, translates the website, launches it, and waits for traffic that never quite arrives — because translation isn't localisation, and a misconfigured hreflang tag can quietly cannibalise rankings across markets without anyone noticing for months.

We've managed SEO for a Fortune 500 company across 40+ countries, which means we've seen the failure modes up close: duplicate content penalties from incorrect canonicals, the wrong country version ranking in the wrong market, keyword research that assumed search behaviour translates directly when it doesn't.

Doing this properly means treating each market as its own research project — its own keywords, its own competitors, its own search habits — while keeping the technical infrastructure clean enough that Google understands the relationship between every version of your site.
