Most businesses come to us after a previous SEO effort didn't deliver — an agency ran an audit eighteen months ago, handed over a PDF, and nothing actually changed. The problem usually isn't the audit itself. It's that audits get treated as a one-off deliverable instead of the starting point for ongoing work.

We run audits differently. Every issue we flag comes with an honest assessment of its actual impact — not a generic severity score copied from a tool. Some technical issues that look alarming in a crawl report barely affect rankings. Some that look minor are costing you significant traffic. We tell you which is which.

The audit covers four areas: technical health, content quality, site architecture and backlink profile. We use the same tools most agencies use — Screaming Frog, Ahrefs, GSC, GA4 — but the difference is in how we read the data and what we recommend doing about it.

FAQ

Common questions

A thorough SEO audit covers technical health (crawlability, page speed, Core Web Vitals), on-page optimisation (meta tags, headings, content quality), site architecture, internal linking and backlink profile. We also look at keyword rankings and compare your visibility against key competitors.

A standard site audit takes 1–2 weeks depending on the size of the site. We deliver a prioritised report with every issue, its estimated impact and specific recommendations — not a raw data dump.

You get a prioritised action plan. We can implement the fixes ourselves, hand them to your dev team, or work alongside your existing agency. The report is yours to use however works best.

A full audit once a year is good practice for most sites. If you've done a major redesign, migration or significant content changes, an audit should happen before and after.
