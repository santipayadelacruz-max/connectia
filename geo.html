Why this matters now

Search is changing.
Visibility has to change with it.

When someone asks ChatGPT for the best SEO agency in Spain, or asks Perplexity to compare digital marketing partners in Europe, your brand either appears in the answer or it doesn't. There's no page two. There's no ad slot you can buy. You either get cited or you don't.

This is what GEO — Generative Engine Optimization — addresses. It's the practice of building your brand's presence in a way that AI search engines recognise, trust and cite when answering relevant questions.

The good news is that the foundations of GEO overlap significantly with good SEO and good content marketing. The brands that get cited by AI are the ones with genuine authority, clear positioning and well-structured content. We help you build that — and track whether it's working.

This is a new area and anyone who tells you they have it fully figured out is overselling. What we can do is apply the signals that correlate with AI citations — E-E-A-T content, entity optimisation, structured data, authority building — and monitor your visibility consistently over time.

Questions about GEO

What people ask
about AI search visibility.

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization. It's the practice of optimising your brand's presence so it appears in answers generated by AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini and Claude — rather than just in traditional search results.

Related but distinct. SEO focuses on traditional search engine rankings. GEO is specifically about appearing in AI-generated answers. The foundations overlap — quality content, real authority, good technical setup — but GEO needs additional work around entity optimisation, structured content and citation building.

A growing share of informational searches — especially research and comparison queries — are being answered directly by AI tools without the user ever visiting a website. If your brand isn't in those answers, you're invisible to that traffic. It's the same shift that happened when Google introduced featured snippets, but bigger.
